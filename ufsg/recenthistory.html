Highlights of UF SG's Biggest Scandals

UF SG has had scandal after scandal revealed by The Alligator and other news sources. While everyday corruption and nepotism can be seen by watching the actions of the Senate, Supreme Court and Executive ticket, there are certain issues that are large enough to warrant press coverage.

While UF Administration continues to allow this to happen, it is still important for people, particuarly UF Students, to understand how this government functions. These are a few of the most notable scandals that UF SG has been entrenched in.


Due to the website creator's personal involvement in the current indie party/caucus, no scandals past Fall 2017 shall be listed, to avoid conflict of interest.

1995-2002 Florida Blue Key Defamation

While it may now be older, this particular case had serious real-world repercussions. In 1995, UF student Charles Grapski ran for Student Body President. It would not end well.

Florida Blue Key, an exclusive honor society that is considered a pipeline into Florida politics, was known to back what was considered “The System” party on campus. What happened next was best described by The Orlando Sentinel in 1998:


“Two days before the vote, Grapski woke up to find the campus plastered with yellow fliers asking, "Do you want this criminal as your student body president?" The flier was a copy of Grapski's arrest record on drunken driving and other charges. Someone, though, had altered the document to add two false charges: sexual battery on a minor and lewd and lascivious conduct. Grapski, who was working with sexual-abuse victims at the time, lost the election. The winner: the candidate for the Blue Key-backed Focus Party.”


In 1998, Grapski won his defamation suit and was awarded $250,000 in damages from a jury from Florida Blue Key, $100,000 more than he originally requested in his suit. In 2002, The Gainesville Sun reported on Grapski accusing John McGovern (One of the individuals reportedly involved in the scandal) of filing for bankruptcy as a way to avoid paying $100,000 that he owed Grapski from the initial suit.

While this case may have been focused on an individual, the information that came out about the organization FBK is still referenced and believed to be true to this today. The case was mentioned by The Alligator when discussing who the organization decides to tap. Additionally, this 2014 Alligator article has a good recap of the entire situation and why it’s relevant today.

Fall 2008 Green Means Go

In the “Green Means Go” Scandal, emails showed SG System leadership exchanging private communications to keep the then-independent party, Orange and Blue Party, out of the SG committees. Attempts to remove one of the four student leaders involved in the collusion, Student Body President Reilly, failed in Senate.

The Fine Print also references this scandal, along with other corrupt FBK practices, in this comprehensive 2009 article.

Spring 2010 Unite Party Tapes

The Unite Party Tapes are 3 recordings from a meeting of the then-System party, Unite, in between the 2 days of voting. Speakers discuss campaign tactics such as changing the “I Voted” stickers (something that the supposedly non-partisan Supervisor of Elections is in charge of), removing the then-independent party Student Alliance’s campaign material, forcing Greek students to vote by withholding food and alcohol and other problematic plans.


“Student Alliance, this is ridiculous, these people don’t have friends. Like you think I’m kidding, these guys don’t have friends. It’s not funny, like these kids are the kids that want to take down the Greek system.”


This was more confirmation of things that had been said before- students are forced to vote by collecting stickers and The System Party claiming the Independent Party is anti-greek (no proof of that claim has been shown in researching for this website). It also confirmed the general sentiment that Greek students set up The System specifically to keep themselves in power.


“I cannot let the Greek system down. You guys cannot let the Greek system down.”


Listen to the tapes on The Alligator, read the transcript on The Fine Print and then read about why these tapes matter so much.

Spring 2013 Sketchy But Do It

In an email from Swamp Party/ fraternity house liaison Leah Miller, more documented proof of the system was revealed. This email stated that fraternities needed to collect “I Voted” stickers from all members and to provide Swamp Party (the then-System Party) with a $250 check. Leah Miller’s email said:


“It is done every semester so this is nothing new, your treasurers should have no problem cutting this.”


Despite the then-independent party, Student’s Party, asking administration to intervene, they were told it wasn’t in admin’s purview. Miller’s email ended with:


“DELETE THIS AFTER YOU GET THE INFORMATION!! WE CANT HAVE ANYTHING ON RECORD! Sketchy, but do it! Thanks!”

Summer 2016 Overturning Online Voting

Despite the fact that the UF Student Body voted to implement online voting, the amendment was overturned in the sketchiest of ways.

As the creator of this website, I was a member of Senate when this happened, and will let the news speak for itself. I will add, for context, that the Supreme Court Meeting where this decision occurred was held the Saturday between Summer A and B (when most students, even those staying for summer, are out of town), and that neither current senators nor The Alligator where informed before it happened. When asked, Senate leadership claimed the meeting had been posted on the Public Notice Board, but it was not there that following week when I checked.

Spring 2017 Student Body President Arrest

Nothing says “Democracy” like a one party system- which UF SG has had far too often. Not only was the executive ticket unopposed, this allowed them to get away with a male-only executive slate.

However, the most notable event that semester was the Student Body President-elect being arrested in Key West over Spring Break- with no repercussions from the University. Not only was this covered locally, but the arrest garnered news coverage across the state, including in The Miami Herald, WUFT, WFLA, NBC Miami and more. It even made it to The New York Post. His arrest video is on YouTube. There was also an online petition to remove him from his position.
