How to stay informed because UF SG Matters

UF Student Government is in charge of nearly $21 million. University of Floridaâ€™s Student Government, founded in 1909, currently has a budget of $20,411,352 (as of Spring 2019). Every credit hour a student pays, they pay $19.06 towards that budget in their Student Activity and Service Fee. But who is actually in charge of that money? If you aren't paying attention, you won't know.

So it makes sense when people are unhappy with how Student Government is run. For example, in Fall 2011 when the Senate didn't impeach the Supervisor of Elections despite the fact that she had failed to follow eight out of 13 rules she was supposed to do to fulfill her job.

The best way to combat a corrupt system like this is to be informed and vote. While The System doesn't love making information accessible- and has even thrown out copies of The Alligator when the Independent party gets an endoresment- following The Alligator and UF SG social media is a good start. You should also glance at the Public Notice Board- located on the Reitz Union Ground Floor by the orange elevators- where SG meeting times (such as Senate and different committees) are announced a minimum of 24 hours in advance.
