Deep Dives into UF SG UF Grad Student's Theses

UF SG is so notable that it has caught the attention of UF Graduate students to the point where students have dedicated projects (including this wesbite) and their full theses to it.

College of Journalism and Communications

Joshua David Aubuchon, a UF College of Journalism and Communications Masters of Mass Communication student, published his thesis in 2005. His work focused on the media framing by The Independent Florida Alligator in the 2004 election.

To read or download this full thesis, go here .

The Bradshaw Papers

David Bradshaw, a Political Science Graduate Student in the College of Liberal Arts and Science, published his thesis in 2013. While obtaining a full copy isn't as easy as an online search (you'll have to reach out to someone who already has a copy), it is referenced and discussed extensivly among those in the independent movement at UF.

The initial Alligator story reporting on the release and significance of this thesis can be found here. The thesis is also referenced in future articles here when discussing UF SG nepotism.

Other sites, such as Her Campus, also cite the thesis when explaining how UF SG functions. This Reddit thread also demonstrates the thesis as fairly common knowledge to anyone who has spent time researching UF SG.

Data Science

Tyler Richards, another UF Student, took a deep dive into the statistics side of SG. He took data from past elections and put it into a predictive model. He also references David Bradshaw's thesis. His analysis, found here, is a great source for those new to UF SG looking to understand "The System" from a more technical side.
